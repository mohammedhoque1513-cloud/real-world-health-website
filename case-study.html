Case Study

Improving Antibiotic Use in Primary Care

Reducing unnecessary prescriptions through evidence-based intervention design.

Client NHS
Location United Kingdom
Year 2024

Overview

Supporting better prescribing decisions at system and practice level.

Real World Health partnered with NHS stakeholders to address persistent variation in antibiotic prescribing across primary care. The objective was to combine rigorous analysis with practical service design so clinical teams could act on evidence more consistently.

The programme focused on understanding prescribing behaviour in context: how clinicians accessed guidance, where local processes created friction, and which interventions could realistically fit existing workflows across a diverse network of GP practices.

By bringing together data insight, behavioural design, and delivery support, we helped create a scalable approach that balanced stewardship goals with frontline feasibility and measurable impact.

The Challenge

Improving stewardship without adding friction to frontline care.

Prescribing improvement required more than new guidance alone. The challenge was to translate evidence into interventions that clinicians would trust, adopt, and sustain under real service pressures.

  • Variation in practice

    Prescribing patterns differed significantly across practices, creating inconsistent patient experience and uneven stewardship performance.

  • Limited visibility of data

    Teams lacked simple, timely views of prescribing behaviour that could support local decision-making and targeted action.

  • Need for practical tools

    Any intervention had to align with existing consultation workflows and avoid increasing administrative burden on clinicians.

Our Approach

A focused delivery model from insight to implementation.

Diagnostic Baseline

Combined prescribing and operational data to identify priority variation, opportunity areas, and local delivery constraints.

Clinical Co-Design

Shaped interventions with clinical leaders and practice teams to ensure credibility, usability, and adoption.

Practice Enablement

Introduced clear prompts, guidance, and support materials that could be embedded into day-to-day consultation workflows.

Outcome Tracking

Measured change continuously to support iteration, demonstrate progress, and reinforce prescribing behaviour improvements.

Impact

Measured improvements communicated with clarity.
