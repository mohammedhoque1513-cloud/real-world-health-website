Overview

Helping leaders improve outcomes across systems, services, and populations.

Our Public & Population Health work supports organisations facing complex questions around prevention, service redesign, prioritisation, and implementation. We bring together evidence review, stakeholder engagement, programme design, and performance measurement to help teams act with confidence.

The result is advice that is analytically robust, operationally realistic, and framed for senior decision-makers responsible for outcomes across regions, systems, or institutions.

Relevant experience includes work with organisations such as the NHS, WHO, IBM, Novartis, UNICEF, and the University of Cambridge.

Our Approach

A delivery model that keeps strategy credible in real-world conditions.

We start by clarifying the decision to be made, the population outcomes at stake, and the practical constraints shaping delivery. From there we build an evidence base, align stakeholders around feasible options, and support implementation planning that is robust enough for executive review and grounded enough for programme teams.

Phase 1

Assess and frame

Understand need, variation, and strategic context.

Phase 2

Generate options

Translate evidence into plausible interventions, models, or policy choices.

Phase 3

Design for adoption

Shape the work for operational fit, stakeholder trust, and practical implementation.

Phase 4

Measure impact

Define the indicators, governance, and learning loops needed to sustain progress.
